Toptracer

Ball-tracking on the range with practice tools, games, and virtual golf—without a countdown clock on your bay.

"We're changing golf. One shot at a time."

What is Toptracer?

One system, many ways to play and improve

Toptracer is a ball-tracking system that changes how golfers practice—whether you want to play a famous course virtually, compete in a long-drive grid, or study launch data on every shot.

Every mode can be used for practice; Toptracer also includes dedicated practice tools that deliver the feedback you need to lower scores. Explore modes below, then visit the range to try them in person.

Practice

Built for feedback

Although every mode can be used as a practice tool, Toptracer offers specific modes designed to give golfers the feedback they need.

Warm-up mode

Instant feedback on every shot: total distance, carry, flat carry, ball speed, launch, curve, height, hang time, landing angle, and proximity to target. Turn off roll, view the range map from your bay, and dial in feel before a round.

My practice

Builds on Warm-up with running averages per club, plus consistency and accuracy vs. a target line shown as a flag on the range. Use the Toptracer app to save sessions and track progress over time.

Toptracer 30

Thirty varied shots—from driver to short wedge—with increasing challenge. The system summarizes strengths and weaknesses, shows results by yardage band, and suggests a handicap based on your performance.

Play

Games and virtual golf

From long drive to stroke play on world-famous courses, Toptracer has a game mode for your group—up to about four players per bay, with bays that are not timed like most simulators.

Virtual golf

Play iconic courses from the range with up to four people. Choose scramble, match play, or stroke play; set handicaps and tees; and enjoy a relaxed pace on untimed bays.

How it looks on screen

Approach shot challenge

Nine approach shots where one miss can cost you—great for dialing mid-irons and wedges under pressure.

The points game

Like darts for golf: land closer to the flag (especially from farther out) for more points, with multipliers and the chance to steal points from opponents.

Long drive

Swing for the fences inside the grid—the longest shot wins. Facility bragging rights included.

Go fish

Team game, twenty shots: any registered shot scores. Great for kids and casual groups—land in the mini pools for the biggest “catches.”

Driving challenge

Nine tee shots blending distance and accuracy—one wild drive can tank the card, similar in spirit to the approach challenge.

Closest to the pin

Classic nearest-the-flag contests on famous virtual holes—simple, competitive, and fun for all skill levels.

Virtual golf

What you see in the bay

Real course graphics, live yardages, and range targets tied to the hole you are playing—here is how two common on-screen layouts work when you tee it up virtually at the range.

Playing a hole (course view)

  • To pin shows remaining distance and elevation change so you can plan carry and rollout.
  • Aim line on the 3D fairway is where you want the shot to start; when your real ball on the range starts toward the virtual flag, flight follows that aim line.
  • Overhead map shows green contours and hole strategy—you can adjust aim from that view as well.
  • Flag / yardage ring in the lower bar is the active target; change it anytime to move your aiming point.

Targets on the range (approach / par mode)

  • Only a few real-world range flags appear on screen; rings around a flag mean it is eligible to play toward.
  • The goal is to land the ball on the fly inside the highlighted inner circle around the chosen flag.
  • The score zones legend maps where the ball finishes to par, bogey, double bogey, and so on from that distance.
  • Use scorecard, rehit, and concede options plus the game clock to keep league or casual rounds moving.
